Professor Jun Zhang

Fellow, IEEE | Highly Cited Researcher

Professor Zhang obtained his PhD degree in Electrical Engineering from the City University of Hong Kong in 2002. Professor Zhang's research contributions include over 300 peer-reviewed publications, with more than 200 appearing in prestigious IEEE Transactions journals. His primary research focus is on Evolutionary Computation, along with interests in Computational Intelligence and Power Electronic Circuits.

Currently, Professor Zhang serves as an associate editor for both the IEEE Transactions on Artificial Intelligence and the IEEE Transactions on Cybernetics.

Research Areas

Professor Jun Zhang specializes in Computational Intelligence with focus on the following research directions:

Evolutionary Computation

Professor Zhang pioneered the Matrix-Based Evolutionary Computation (MEC) theoretical framework, establishing its mathematical formulation and successfully implementing it with supercomputing resources. His evolutionary algorithms simulate intelligent behaviors found in nature and operate without depending on precise mathematical models, making them suitable for solving complex optimization problems in various domains.

Swarm Intelligence

Professor Zhang's work in swarm intelligence includes techniques such as particle swarm optimization and ant colony optimization. These approaches have demonstrated exceptional performance in applications including traveling salesman problems, vehicle routing, and cloud resource scheduling.

Applications in Complex Environments

Professor Zhang applies computational intelligence approaches to real-world problems in cloud computing and big data environments. His research extends to diverse domains including news communication, finance, and resource optimization. The model-free nature of these approaches enables effective solutions for problems with high modeling complexity.
